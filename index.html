Know Your Bread

Bread School

Every loaf tells a story. Here's what's actually happening in each one — and why it matters for your body.

The Classic

  • Long fermentation = easier digestion
  • Lower glycaemic index than regular bread
  • Natural probiotics from wild yeast

Did you know? The long fermentation process breaks down gluten proteins, making sourdough easier to digest even for some gluten-sensitive people.

The Rye

  • High fibre content — keeps you full longer
  • Supports gut microbiome diversity
  • Lower insulin response than wheat bread

Did you know? Rye contains a unique type of fibre called arabinoxylan that feeds beneficial gut bacteria more effectively than most other grains.

The Protein

  • Macro-friendly — fits any training diet
  • Supports muscle recovery post-workout
  • Satiating — fewer cravings throughout the day

Did you know? Higher protein content per slice means you get the carb satisfaction of bread while staying closer to your macros — no compromises.

The Manoushe

  • Za'atar is rich in antioxidants & thymol
  • Olive oil provides heart-healthy monounsaturated fats
  • Centuries of Lebanese culinary heritage

Did you know? Za'atar has been used in the Levant for over 2,000 years — not just for flavour, but as a natural antimicrobial and brain booster.

The Spicy One

  • Capsaicin gives a natural metabolism boost
  • Cheddar adds calcium and protein
  • Bold comfort food — elevated

Did you know? Capsaicin — the compound that makes jalapeños hot — has been shown to temporarily increase metabolic rate by up to 8%.

The Sweet One

  • Goat cheese is easier to digest than cow dairy
  • Honey is a natural sweetener with antimicrobial properties
  • Perfectly balanced sweet-savoury contrast

Did you know? Goat milk is naturally homogenised, forming smaller fat globules than cow milk — which makes it gentler on the digestive system.
